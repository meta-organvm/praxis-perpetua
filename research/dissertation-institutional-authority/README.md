## Abstract

This dissertation presents the design, implementation, and theoretical grounding of a self-governing evaluative authority — a domain-agnostic system that attaches to any structured operational pipeline to provide continuous, multi-perspective quality assessment with statistical agreement measurement and recursive self-correction. Drawing on organizational cybernetics (Beer), autopoietic systems theory (Maturana/Varela, Luhmann), psychometric inter-rater reliability (Shrout/Fleiss, Cohen, Fleiss), and the LLM-as-judge literature (Zheng et al.), it argues that persona-driven evaluative disagreement constitutes a governance signal, not noise.
