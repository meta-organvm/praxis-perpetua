## I. Core Meta-Laws of Reality

These are recursive, cross-domain, and appear in all systems: natural, human, informational, spiritual.

**[L1] Law of Causality** — Every effect has a cause. Time flows with consequence. Enables science, narrative, memory, and moral judgment. Symbol: Domino, wave crest, karmic loop.

**[L2] Law of Perception (Maya/Illusion)** — Reality is mediated. All knowledge is filtered through perception, bias, and symbolic frameworks. Basis of art, philosophy, theology, deception. Symbol: Mirror, veil, simulation.

**[L3] Law of Entropy (2nd Law of Thermodynamics)** — Systems tend toward disorder without energy input. Drives evolution, decay, transformation, creativity. Symbol: Rot, chaos, bloom.

**[L4] Law of Recursion / Self-Similarity** — Systems often mirror themselves at multiple scales. Found in fractals, myth cycles, code, DNA, society. Symbol: Spiral, ouroboros, Mandelbrot.

**[L5] Law of Contrast / Division** — Meaning is generated by difference. Nothing can be understood in isolation. Enables logic, language, aesthetics, identity. Symbol: Binary, shadow, dialectic.

**[L6] Law of Connection / Interdependence** — All things are entangled, materially or symbolically. Foundational to ecology, ethics, systems design. Symbol: Web, network, mycelium.

**[L7] Law of Compensation / Conservation** — For every gain, something is lost or transformed; energy is not destroyed, only changed. Undergirds economics, sacrifice, ritual, physics. Symbol: Scales, balance, crucible.

## Cross-Domain Meta-Patterns

Six recurring "laws of laws" that manifest across all domains:

### 1. Duality and Balance
Nearly every domain emphasizes binary or paired forces in balance. Physics: action-reaction. Philosophy: yin-yang, dialectic. Social: balance of powers. Art: symmetry vs. asymmetry. Stability emerges from balancing opposing forces.

### 2. Cause and Effect (Causality)
All systems assume chains of causation. Scientific laws connect cause and effect. Karma applies causality to morality. Chekhov's gun enforces it in storytelling. The principle of sufficient reason: everything that exists must have a reason.

### 3. Recursion and Self-Similarity
"As above, so below." Fractals in math, hero's journey recurring in subplots, historical cycles. Nature builds complexity by repeating simple rules. Self-similar patterns manifest across scales.

### 4. Entropy and Decay vs. Order and Growth
Systems naturally drift toward disorder unless energy or intelligence is applied. Life is order maintained by constant energy use. Civilizations rise (creating order) and fall (to entropy). Maintenance and sustainability as cross-domain needs.

### 5. Emergence and Complexity from Simplicity
Simple rules yield complex outcomes. Mendel's genetics → diversity of life. Newton's equations → planetary motions. Ant colonies: simple individual behaviors yield intelligent colony. Global order arises from local interactions following simple rules.

### 6. Governance and Regulation
From genes regulating biology to separation of powers in government to game mechanics limiting player action — systems develop regulatory mechanisms. Feedback loops (negative for stability, positive for growth) appear from engineering to ecology to economics.

### 7. Ontological Stratification and Level-Crossing
Reality is organized in layers where each layer has laws that cannot be reduced to the layer below. Chemistry emerges from physics but is not predictable by physics alone. Biology emerges from chemistry but obeys laws chemistry cannot anticipate. Sociology emerges from biology but follows patterns biology cannot derive. Confusion of levels — applying one stratum's logic to another stratum's problem — is a systematic source of error across all domains. Physics: quantum mechanics vs. classical mechanics as stratum confusion. Philosophy: the is-ought problem as stratum boundary. Computation: confusing implementation detail with architectural pattern. Governance: applying operational policy to constitutional questions. The productive use of stratification is knowing which level's logic to apply and when to deliberately cross levels (meta-cognition, self-reference, recursive self-improvement).
