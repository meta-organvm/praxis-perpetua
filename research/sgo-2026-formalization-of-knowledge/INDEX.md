## The Grand Arc

This research program traces a single question across seven intellectual territories: **How does knowledge become formal?**

The arc begins with naming -- the most primitive act of formalization, where a sign is bound to a referent and the world becomes speakable. It moves through organizational structure (how named things are arranged), network ontology (how arranged things relate), formal language theory (how relations are expressed in grammars), and unified semantics (how grammars carry meaning). It confronts the impossibility landscape (where formalization encounters its own limits) and measurement theory (how formalized systems can be assessed without corrupting what they measure). It culminates in a capstone that integrates all seven threads into a four-dimensional architecture of meaning.

The program produces **13 formal works**: 7 primary research papers (one per adventure), 5 cross-adventure synthesis papers, and 1 capstone dissertation. Together these constitute a coherent doctoral-level research contribution spanning formal semantics, computability theory, organizational science, philosophy of language, science and technology studies, and psychometrics.
