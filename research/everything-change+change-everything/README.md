## Central Axiom

> *Everything is change && Change everything* — read in both directions simultaneously.

The first reading is ontological: the world is metabolic; nothing persists except through transformation. The second is ethical: given that everything is already changing, the directive is to change it *deliberately*, *completely*, and *well*. The double ampersand (&&) is not decoration — it is a logical AND requiring both propositions to evaluate true. Neither reading is prior. The axiom is a standing wave between observation and imperative.

## Structure

The dissertation is organized as three papers framed by an overture and a coda, totaling 14 chapters. The three-paper dialectical structure follows the model established by SGO-2026-D-003 (thesis / antithesis / synthesis), but with a distinctive signal-routing architecture:

```
OVERTURE          (1 chapter)     — the axiom stated
PAPER I  — THE PRACTITIONER      (4 chapters)   Thesis      — feedback
PAPER II — THE THEORIST          (4 chapters)   Antithesis  — feedsides
PAPER III — THE SYSTEM           (4 chapters)   Synthesis   — feedforward
CODA              (1 chapter)     — the axiom enacted
BIBLIOGRAPHY
```

**The document's structure enacts its argument.** Paper I discovers the metabolic principle through craft; Paper II genealogizes it through intellect; Paper III constitutionalizes it through governance. The three signal routes — feedback, feedsides, feedforward — are themselves metabolic: each transforms the signal it carries rather than merely transmitting it.

## The Three Papers

### Paper I — THE PRACTITIONER (Thesis)

**Voice:** Empirically prior, craft-discovered
**Signal route:** Feedback — the practitioner's hand teaches the practitioner's mind

The metabolic principle as it emerges from making: creative practice, studio formation, material engagement, the body-in-the-loop. Paper I argues that the metabolic principle was *discovered* before it was theorized — that making things taught ORGANVM what change means before any theoretical framework could name it. Evidence drawn from generative art, performance systems, production methodology, and the lived experience of building a creative-institutional system.

### Paper II — THE THEORIST (Antithesis)

**Voice:** Theoretically prior, intellectually genealogized
**Signal route:** Feedsides — the theorist draws from parallel traditions that never intended to converge

The metabolic principle as it descends from intellectual tradition: autopoiesis (Maturana/Varela), process philosophy (Whitehead, Deleuze), systems cybernetics (Beer, Ashby), thermodynamic dissipative structures (Prigogine), alchemical transformation, and recursive self-production. Paper II argues that the metabolic principle was *always already theorized* — that the practitioner's discovery in Paper I was a rediscovery of something intellectual history had articulated in dozens of independent lineages. The antithesis: theory is prior to practice, and the practitioner merely stumbled onto what the theorist already knew.

### Paper III — THE SYSTEM (Synthesis)

**Voice:** Constitutional, binding directives
**Signal route:** Feedforward — the system projects the principle into every future operation

The metabolic principle as prime directive: neither discovered nor inherited but *constituted* as the operational law governing ORGANVM. Paper III resolves the dialectic by arguing that neither practice nor theory is prior — the metabolic principle exists only in the *ongoing act of systemic self-transformation*. It is neither a finding (Paper I) nor a theorem (Paper II) but a constitutional commitment. Paper III issues binding directives that encode the metabolic principle into ORGANVM's governance, promotion machinery, evaluation criteria, and organ architecture.
