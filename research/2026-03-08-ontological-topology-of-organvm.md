# The Ontological Topology of ORGANVM

> Five frameworks for understanding layered systems — Emergence, General Systems Theory, Ontological Stratification, Consilience, and Reductionism vs. Holism — converge on a single question: where does ORGANVM *sit* in the ontological stack, and how do external organizational paradigms live inside, around, on top of, or below it? This document maps the topology and proposes a meta-cognitive layer: the **Study Suite**, a system of observer agents modeled on academia, science, and governance.

## Section I — The Ontological Stack

ORGANVM does not exist at a single level of reality. It spans five strata, each with its own physics, governance logic, and failure modes.

### Layer −2: Substrate

**What it is.** Hardware (Apple Silicon M3, 16GB RAM). Operating system (macOS). Git as content-addressable storage. GitHub as collaboration platform. AI models (Claude, Gemini, Codex) as cognitive compute. Context windows as working memory. Network connectivity. Electricity.

**Governance logic.** Physical constraints propagate upward as hard limits. The 16GB RAM ceiling is not negotiable by architectural decision. Context window sizes are not expandable by governance rule. Substrate failures (power loss, network outage, model unavailability) cascade instantly.

**ORGANVM's relationship.** The system is *hosted* by this layer but does not *control* it. Substrate awareness manifests as defensive design: local-first storage, multi-agent redundancy, session transcript archival. The `.venv/`, `.git/`, and `seed.yaml` contracts are all substrate-adjacent artifacts — they exist at the boundary between substrate and tooling.

### Layer −1: Tooling

**What it is.** The `organvm` CLI (13 command groups). JSON Schemas (`schema-definitions/`). The MCP server (17 tools). Runtimes (Python 3.11+, Node.js). Package managers. CI workflows. The alchemia ingestion pipeline. Ruff, Pyright, pytest.

**Governance logic.** Tooling is governed by *correctness* — does it work as specified? Tests, type checking, and linting enforce tooling-layer contracts. A failing test is a tooling-layer violation, not a governance-layer one.

**ORGANVM's relationship.** The system *uses* this layer and *owns* it. Every tool here was built to serve the architectural and governance layers above. The key insight: tooling changes are cheap and reversible; architectural changes are expensive and path-dependent.

### Layer 0: ORGANVM (The Architecture)

**What it is.** The eight-organ model. The registry (`registry-v2.json`, 103 repos). The governance rules (dependency validation, promotion state machine). The seed contracts (`seed.yaml` produces/consumes edges). The naming conventions (double-hyphen separation). The conductor model (human directs, AI executes). The session lifecycle (FRAME → SHAPE → BUILD → PROVE → DONE).

**Governance logic.** Architectural decisions are governed by *coherence* — does the whole hold together? The unidirectional dependency flow (I → II → III) is an architectural invariant, not a tooling preference. The promotion state machine is an architectural feature, not a CI check.

**ORGANVM's relationship.** This IS the system. The architecture is what makes ORGANVM ORGANVM, as distinct from "a person with a lot of repos." The registry is the identity. The governance rules are the constitution. The seed contracts are the social contract between repos.

### Layer +1: Emergent Properties

**What it is.** Institutional coherence — the sense that 105 repos are one thing, not 105 things. Brand identity across 8 GitHub organizations. Portfolio effect — the whole is more impressive than the sum. Knowledge accumulation across the research corpus (40+ documents, 404K+ words). Compound credibility from sustained operation. The derived principles (Y1–Y5, S1–S5, C1–C4, A1–A4, E1–E2) as crystallized institutional memory.

**Governance logic.** Emergent properties cannot be *commanded* into existence. They are governed *indirectly* — by maintaining the conditions from which they arise. Anti-stagnation governance at Layer 0 creates the conditions for institutional coherence at Layer +1. The omega scorecard attempts to *measure* emergence but cannot *cause* it.

**ORGANVM's relationship.** The system *produces* this layer but cannot fully *predict* it. The research corpus achieving consilience across 40+ documents was not planned — it emerged from consistent methodology applied over time. This is exactly what the Emergence framework describes: simple rules at one level producing complex behavior at the next.

### Layer +2: Environment

**What it is.** The creative technology industry. The AI tooling ecosystem. Academic institutions and grant bodies. Other solo practitioners and micro-institutions. Regulatory frameworks (IP law, EU AI Act). Cultural attitudes toward AI, automation, and solo practice. Economic conditions. The job market.

**Governance logic.** The environment is governed by forces ORGANVM does not control. Market shifts, regulatory changes, and cultural movements are exogenous. The system can *adapt* to environmental changes but cannot *prevent* them.

**ORGANVM's relationship.** The system is *embedded in* this layer and *exchanges* with it. Products (Layer 0) enter the market (Layer +2). Grant applications (Layer +1 credibility) seek institutional funding (Layer +2). Essays (Layer 0, ORGAN-V) participate in cultural discourse (Layer +2). The [Bootstrap-to-Scale](2026-03-07-bootstrap-to-scale-bloom.md) analysis is explicitly a study of how creative institutions navigate this layer.

### The Stack as a Whole

The five layers are not merely descriptive — they are *causally ordered*. Changes propagate upward (substrate failures crash everything; tooling bugs corrupt architecture; architectural incoherence destroys emergent properties). Influence propagates downward more slowly and less reliably (environmental market signals eventually reshape architecture, which reshapes tooling, which requires different substrate). The human conductor is the agent who translates between layers — perceiving environmental signals at +2, making architectural decisions at 0, and directing tooling work at −1.

## Section II — Framework-by-Framework Analysis

### 1. Emergence

**General principle.** Complex, unpredictable properties arise from the interaction of simpler components following local rules. The whole is not merely greater than the sum of its parts — it is *different in kind*. (Cross-reference: Meta-Laws Codex, Meta-Pattern #5 — "Simple rules yield complex outcomes.")

**ORGANVM mapping.** Each repo follows simple local contracts: a `seed.yaml` declaring its edges, a promotion status, a tier. No single repo "knows about" the institutional portfolio effect. Yet the aggregate — 105 repos across 8 organs with governed dependencies — produces something no single repo contains: a coherent creative institution. The derived principles (Y1–Y5) are themselves emergent — they were not designed but *discovered* through post-session review.

**Actionable prescription: Emergence Detectors.** The system currently lacks explicit mechanisms for recognizing emergent properties. Proposed: a "cross-organ coherence metric" that measures how many inter-organ edges are active (producing/consuming real artifacts, not just declared). When coherence exceeds a threshold, the system has achieved genuine emergence — not just aspiration. This metric belongs in the omega scorecard as a criterion: "Cross-organ edges producing measurable artifacts."

### 2. General Systems Theory

**General principle.** A system is defined by its boundary (what's in vs. out), its inputs and outputs across that boundary, and its internal feedback loops. Systems maintain identity through homeostasis — negative feedback loops that counteract perturbation. (Cross-reference: Meta-Laws Codex, [C3] Law of Requisite Variety, [C7] Feedback Loops.)

**ORGANVM mapping.** The registry IS the boundary — if a repo is in `registry-v2.json`, it's in the system. Alchemia is the input function (intake → absorb → alchemize: external material enters the system). Products, essays, and distribution (ORGANs III, V, VII) are the output functions. The promotion state machine is a negative feedback loop: repos that stagnate are not promoted; repos that fail governance checks are blocked. The session lifecycle (FRAME → SHAPE → BUILD → PROVE → DONE) is a negative feedback loop within individual work sessions.

**Actionable prescription: Feedback Loop Inventory.** Negative feedback loops (stagnation prevention, governance gates) are well-mapped. *Positive* feedback loops — the mechanisms by which success at one point amplifies success elsewhere — are unmapped and ungoverned. Examples of unmapped positive loops: a successful product (ORGAN-III) generating case study material (ORGAN-V) generating community interest (ORGAN-VI) generating distribution (ORGAN-VII) generating more product users. These virtuous cycles exist implicitly but are not tracked, measured, or protected. An explicit positive feedback loop map would identify which loops to invest in and which might become runaway growth that outpaces governance capacity.

### 3. Ontological Stratification

**General principle.** Reality is organized in layers, and each layer has its own laws that cannot be reduced to the layer below. Chemistry has laws that physics cannot predict. Biology has laws that chemistry cannot predict. Sociology has laws that biology cannot predict. Confusion of levels — applying the wrong layer's logic to a problem — is a systematic source of error. (Cross-reference: Morphodynamics of Assembly, the transition from physics to biology to sociology of organizational bodies.)

**ORGANVM mapping.** The five-layer stack (Section I) is ORGANVM's ontological stratification made explicit. The crucial operational insight is that decisions must respect their stratum:

| Decision Type | Stratum | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Implementation detail | Layer −1 (Tooling) | "Use ruff instead of flake8" |
| Architectural pattern | Layer 0 (Architecture) | "ORGAN-III products must declare revenue_model" |
| Governance rule | Layer 0 (Architecture) | "Promotion requires CI passing" |
| Philosophical commitment | Layer +1 (Emergent) | "Human friction is a feature, not a bug" (Y5) |

Applying tooling-layer logic to a philosophical commitment ("let's add a CI check to enforce Y5") is a category error. Applying philosophical-commitment logic to a tooling choice ("our choice of linter reflects our deepest values") is equally erroneous. Both cause paralysis.

**Actionable prescription: Stratum Labels.** Every governance decision, SOP, and derived principle should be tagged with its stratum. This prevents the common failure of treating a philosophical commitment as if it were a CI check, or treating a CI check as if it were a philosophical commitment. The four strata: `implementation-detail`, `architectural-pattern`, `governance-rule`, `philosophical-commitment`.

### 4. Consilience

**General principle.** Knowledge achieves maximum reliability when independent lines of evidence from different domains converge on the same conclusion. A finding confirmed by physics AND biology AND sociology is more robust than one confirmed by physics alone. Edward O. Wilson's "unity of knowledge" — the interlocking of causal explanations across disciplines. (Cross-reference: Meta-Laws Codex, the very existence of 7 core meta-laws that appear across all 9 domains.)

**ORGANVM mapping.** The research corpus IS a consilience engine. The finding that "governance must be codified before scaling pressures arrive" is confirmed by:
- Case study analysis: 15+ creative institutions in [Bootstrap-to-Scale](2026-03-07-bootstrap-to-scale-bloom.md)
- Systems theory: [Metaphysics of Flux](2026-03-07-metaphysics-of-flux.md) on autopoiesis requiring self-referential governance
- Organizational physics: [Morphodynamics of Assembly](2026-01-assembling-disparate-units.md) on conflict suppression preceding stable assembly
- Direct experience: derived principle Y2 ("Governance is soil, not bureaucracy")

Four independent sources — case study, theory, physics analogy, lived experience — converging on one principle. This is consilience in action.

**Actionable prescription: Consilience Index.** For each derived principle, track how many independent sources confirm it and from how many distinct domains. A principle with a consilience index of 1 (single source, single domain) is a hypothesis. A principle with index 4+ (multiple sources, multiple domains) is approaching law. This metric would guide which principles to invest in (high-consilience principles deserve SOPs and enforcement) and which to hold lightly (low-consilience principles are provisional).

### 5. Reductionism vs. Holism

**General principle.** Reductionism decomposes wholes into parts to understand mechanisms. Holism insists that wholes have properties their parts lack. Neither is wrong; each is incomplete without the other. The tension between them is productive — reductionism without holism loses meaning; holism without reductionism loses rigor. (Cross-reference: Meta-Laws Codex, [B7] The Hermeneutic Circle — "Understanding the whole depends on understanding the parts, and vice versa.")

**ORGANVM mapping.** The omega scorecard is a 17-bit reduction of institutional health. Each bit answers a yes/no question. This is reductionism at its most explicit — and at its most useful. Without it, "how healthy is the system?" is unanswerable. With it, the answer is "4/17 criteria met" — legible, trackable, actionable.

But the omega scorecard cannot capture institutional coherence, brand identity, or portfolio effect. These are holistic properties — they exist in the gestalt, not in any individual criterion. The [Systemic Project-Product Analysis](2026-03-07-systemic-project-analysis.md) addresses this tension with its two-lane model (Lane A: does the thing work? Lane B: can the world find, trust, and pay for it?), where Lane A is reductive and Lane B is holistic.

**Actionable prescription: Reduction Disclaimer Pattern.** Every quantitative score, metric, or index produced by the system should carry an explicit disclaimer of what it *cannot* capture. The omega scorecard README should state: "This scorecard reduces institutional health to 17 binary criteria. It cannot measure: institutional coherence, brand perception, knowledge accumulation quality, or community trust." This is not false modesty — it is epistemic hygiene that prevents Goodhart's Law ("When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure").

## Section III — Collective Synthesis

Four insights that no single framework produces alone:

### Insight 1: The Conductor Operates at Every Stratum Simultaneously

The human conductor (principle Y4: "The director perceives; specialists execute") is unique in the system because they cross all five layers. They troubleshoot substrate issues (RAM pressure), configure tooling (CLI commands), make architectural decisions (organ boundaries), recognize emergent properties (portfolio coherence), and navigate the environment (grant applications, job market). No AI agent crosses strata this way — each is specialized to one or two layers. This is not a limitation of AI but a *feature* of the conductor model: the human's cross-stratum perception IS the system's adaptive controller.

### Insight 2: Positive Feedback Loops Are the System's Greatest Unmapped Risk

General Systems Theory identifies unmapped positive feedback loops as dangerous. Emergence theory explains why: emergent properties can be destructive as well as constructive. An unmapped positive feedback loop — say, a viral essay generating more community engagement than the governance system can process — could produce emergent chaos rather than emergent coherence. The current architecture has extensive negative feedback (governance gates, WIP limits, promotion requirements) but almost no explicit positive feedback governance. This is the single largest architectural gap this analysis reveals.

### Insight 3: Consilience Legitimizes the Research Corpus as a Knowledge System

The research corpus (40+ documents, 404K+ words) is not "just documentation." Through the lens of consilience, it is an epistemically structured knowledge system where independent sources from independent domains converge on shared principles. The derived principles (Y1–Y5, S1–S5, etc.) are the crystallized output of this consilience process. This reframes the corpus from an operational artifact to an intellectual contribution — it is, in miniature, what E. O. Wilson proposed for the sciences: a unified framework where insights from physics, philosophy, organizational theory, and lived experience interlock.

### Insight 4: The System Can Study Itself Without Circularity

Ontological stratification resolves the apparent paradox of self-study. A system studying itself at the *same stratum* is circular (the map IS the territory). But a system at Layer 0 being observed by agents at Layer +1 is not circular — it is a higher-order observation of a lower-order phenomenon, exactly as sociology observes biology without becoming biology. This insight opens the door to Section IV.

## Section IV — The Study Suite: Parallel Organizational Systems as Agent Workstreams

### The Core Proposition

Real-world organizational systems — academia, scientific method, governance — do not merely *describe* ORGANVM. They can be **literally instantiated** as parallel AI agent workstreams that observe, critique, review, and generate knowledge about the system while it operates.

This is not metaphor. The academic system has concrete operations (peer review, citation tracking, literature synthesis). The scientific system has concrete operations (hypothesis formulation, experiment design, falsification). The governance system has concrete operations (compliance auditing, drift detection, judicial review). Each of these can be implemented as a persistent agent role with defined inputs, outputs, and triggering conditions.

The existing infrastructure already contains proto-versions of each:

- **E2G reviews** are manual academic-style peer review sessions — a human directing an AI to critique the system's own code and documentation
- **Omega scorecard criteria** are testable hypotheses — binary propositions about system health that are confirmed or refuted by evidence
- **SOP--autopoietic-systems-diagnostics.md** Phase II defines "observer position" auditing — the governance system in embryonic form
- **Session transcript archive** (1,367 sessions / 3.5GB) is the raw corpus that a study agent would analyze

The Study Suite formalizes these into three persistent agent roles.

### Agent 1: The Reviewer (Academic System)

**Organizational parallel.** The academic system: peer review, citation tracking, literature synthesis, journal publication.

**Agent role.** The Reviewer reads session transcripts, research documents, and code changes. It identifies:
- **Consilience**: independent sessions arriving at the same conclusion without referencing each other
- **Contradiction**: sessions or documents that assert incompatible claims
- **Gaps**: domains or questions addressed in early sessions but never followed up
- **Drift**: gradual shift in terminology, methodology, or priorities across sessions

**Input sources.** Session transcript archive, research corpus, derived principles, commit history.

**Output artifacts.**
- Periodic "findings journal" entries in `praxis-perpetua/studies/` (new directory)
- Proposed updates to `derived-principles.md` (never applied directly — proposed for human review)
- Consilience index updates for each derived principle
- Citation graph across research documents (which documents reference which)

**Existing hooks.**
- E2G reviews ([evaluation-to-growth](../lessons/derived-principles.md)) are the manual version; the Reviewer agent automates the *observation* layer while preserving human judgment for the *action* layer
- `SOP--session-self-critique.md` defines post-session review; the Reviewer extends this from single-session retrospective to cross-session longitudinal analysis
- The session intelligence system (`organvm session analyze`, `organvm session plans --audit`) provides the CLI infrastructure for cross-session analysis

**Scheduling.** Event-triggered: post-session (after every session that produces or modifies research documents). Periodic: weekly synthesis across all sessions from the prior week.

### Agent 2: The Experimenter (Scientific System)

**Organizational parallel.** The scientific method: hypothesis formulation, experimental design, controlled testing, falsification, reproducibility.

**Agent role.** The Experimenter treats derived principles and architectural decisions as *hypotheses* and designs tests to confirm or refute them. It:
- Extracts testable predictions from derived principles (e.g., Y2 "Governance is soil" predicts that repos with premature governance should show slower progress than peers)
- Designs measurement protocols using existing metrics infrastructure
- Tracks confirmation/refutation over time
- Proposes new omega scorecard criteria based on confirmed hypotheses

**Input sources.** Derived principles, omega scorecard, registry data, metrics timeseries, session outcomes.

**Output artifacts.**
- Hypothesis registry in `praxis-perpetua/studies/hypotheses/` (structured YAML: hypothesis, prediction, test, status, evidence)
- Confirmation/refutation reports
- Proposed new omega criteria (never applied directly — proposed for human review)
- Reproducibility notes: which findings hold when tested against different time periods or different organ subsets

**Existing hooks.**
- Omega scorecard criteria are already testable hypotheses; `_KNOWN_MET` in `scorecard.py` is the manual confirmation mechanism
- Metrics timeseries (`metrics/timeseries.py`) provides historical data for longitudinal testing
- The Living Data Organism (`metrics/organism.py`) provides the "vital signs" that many hypotheses would test against

**Scheduling.** Periodic: monthly hypothesis review cycle. Event-triggered: post-promotion (test whether promoted repos exhibit predicted behaviors).

### Agent 3: The Auditor (Governance System)

**Organizational parallel.** The governance system: legislative compliance, regulatory enforcement, judicial review, constitutional interpretation.

**Agent role.** The Auditor continuously validates that the system's actual state matches its declared governance rules. It:
- Detects drift between `governance-rules.json` declarations and actual dependency graph
- Flags repos whose `seed.yaml` edges don't match their actual imports/interactions
- Monitors for governance rule violations that slipped through CI
- Interprets ambiguous governance situations by reference to derived principles (the "constitutional interpretation" function)

**Input sources.** `governance-rules.json`, `registry-v2.json`, all `seed.yaml` files, git commit history, CI results, derived principles.

**Output artifacts.**
- Drift reports in `praxis-perpetua/studies/audits/`
- Violation alerts (with severity classification: informational, warning, critical)
- "Judicial opinions" — reasoned interpretations of how a derived principle applies to a specific ambiguous case
- Proposed governance rule updates (never applied directly — proposed for human review)

**Existing hooks.**
- `SOP--autopoietic-systems-diagnostics.md` Phase II (observer position) defines the auditor's function in procedural terms
- `SOP--structural-integrity-audit.md` defines the audit methodology; the Auditor agent automates continuous execution
- `organvm governance audit` CLI command performs point-in-time audits; the Auditor extends this to continuous monitoring
- `organvm governance check-deps` validates dependency rules; the Auditor extends this to semantic-level edge validation

**Scheduling.** Continuous: post-commit hooks on governance-relevant files. Periodic: weekly full audit. Event-triggered: post-promotion (verify that the promoted repo meets all tier requirements).

### Design Principles for the Study Suite

**1. Observers, not actors.** Study Suite agents produce knowledge artifacts (reports, findings, proposed principles, drift reports) but **never modify production data**. They do not update `registry-v2.json`, do not modify `seed.yaml` files, do not alter governance rules. The human conductor reviews their output and decides what to act on. This is principle Y4 applied to the meta-cognitive layer: the director perceives (reads study reports); specialists execute (the conductor acts on findings).

**2. Outputs live in `praxis-perpetua/studies/`.** A new directory structure:
```
praxis-perpetua/studies/
  findings/          # Reviewer output: consilience, contradiction, gap reports
  hypotheses/        # Experimenter output: hypothesis registry and test results
  audits/            # Auditor output: drift reports, violation alerts, opinions
```
All outputs are append-only dated files. No output is ever overwritten — new findings supersede old ones by date, mirroring the session log convention.

**3. No production data access.** Study Suite agents read production data (registry, seeds, governance rules) but write only to `praxis-perpetua/studies/`. This is enforced by file-path scoping in agent configuration, not by trust.

**4. Triggered, not polled.** Where possible, Study Suite agents are triggered by events (post-session, post-commit, post-promotion) rather than running on fixed schedules. This respects the 16GB RAM constraint and avoids unnecessary computation.

**5. Human-readable output.** All Study Suite output is markdown — no JSON-only reports, no binary artifacts. The human conductor must be able to read any output directly. This follows the principle that the system serves the human, not the other way around.

### Relationship to Existing Agent Architecture

The Study Suite is fundamentally different from existing agent workstreams:

- `SOP--agent-seeding-and-workforce-planning.md` governs **execution agents** — agents that build, deploy, write, and modify. The Study Suite governs **observation agents** — agents that read, analyze, and report.
- `SOP--cross-agent-handoff.md` defines how execution agents pass work between each other. Study Suite agents do not participate in execution handoffs — they observe them.
- The E2G review pattern is the closest existing analogy, but E2G reviews are human-initiated and session-scoped. The Study Suite is event-triggered and cross-session.

This separation is critical: observation and execution must not be entangled. An agent that both audits governance AND modifies governance is a fox guarding the henhouse. The Study Suite's observer-only constraint is a structural guarantee, not a policy preference.

### What This Section Is and Is Not

This is the **founding specification** — enough to define the agent roles, their inputs and outputs, their triggering conditions, and their relationship to existing infrastructure. It is NOT a full implementation plan. The implementation details (which AI model for each agent, exact prompt engineering, scheduling infrastructure, output schema design) are a separate initiative. This document plants the seed; a future session cultivates it into running code.

## Section V — Proposed Derived Principles

### Y6: The system is ontologically stratified; decisions must respect their stratum.

Four strata — `implementation-detail`, `architectural-pattern`, `governance-rule`, `philosophical-commitment` — each with its own logic, timescale, and reversibility. Applying a lower stratum's logic to a higher-stratum decision causes recklessness (treating philosophical commitments as if they were config flags). Applying a higher stratum's logic to a lower-stratum decision causes paralysis (treating linter choice as if it were a moral commitment).

**Test:** Before any decision, ask: which stratum does this belong to? If the decision mechanism doesn't match the stratum, recalibrate.

**Source:** This document (ontological topology analysis, Section I + Section II §3).

### Y7: Every organizational system that describes the architecture can be instantiated as a living agent within it.

The academic system becomes a Reviewer agent. The scientific system becomes an Experimenter agent. The governance system becomes an Auditor agent. The map becomes the territory when the map has agency. This is not metaphor — it is a design principle: any organizational paradigm that produces actionable knowledge about the system can be formalized as an observer agent with defined inputs, outputs, and triggering conditions.

**Constraint:** Instantiated organizational systems must be *observers, not actors*. They produce knowledge; the human conductor acts on it. The moment an observer agent modifies what it observes, it ceases to be a trustworthy observer (Heisenberg at the organizational level).

**Source:** This document (ontological topology analysis, Section IV — Study Suite founding specification). ## Epilogue: The Self-Studying System

ORGANVM is, by design, a system that describes itself. The registry describes the repos. The governance rules describe the promotion logic. The seed contracts describe the dependency graph. The research corpus describes the philosophy. This document adds one more layer: a description of the system's position in the ontological stack and a proposal for making that description *active* — not merely a document to be read, but a specification for agents that continuously observe, test, and audit.

The five frameworks converge on a single meta-insight: **the system's capacity to study itself is not a luxury — it is a survival mechanism**. Systems that cannot observe their own emergent properties (Emergence), that lack feedback on their own feedback loops (General Systems Theory), that confuse their own strata (Ontological Stratification), that cannot cross-validate their own conclusions (Consilience), and that mistake their metrics for their reality (Reductionism vs. Holism) — these systems fail. Not dramatically, but quietly, through accumulated drift.
