### Scoring Guide

**Currency** (Is it timely?)
- 9-10: Published within 2 years; field moves fast
- 7-8: Published within 5 years; still cited actively
- 5-6: Published within 10 years; foundational but aging
- 3-4: Over 10 years; included only as seminal work
- 1-2: Outdated; contradicted by subsequent research

**Relevance** (Does it address your research question?)
- 9-10: Directly addresses primary research question
- 7-8: Addresses a sub-theme or adjacent concern
- 5-6: Provides useful context or background
- 3-4: Tangential; included for completeness
- 1-2: Marginal relevance; likely exclude

**Authority** (Who wrote it?)
- 9-10: Leading scholar in field; highly cited; top-tier institution
- 7-8: Established researcher; peer-reviewed journal
- 5-6: Credentialed author; reputable publisher
- 3-4: Emerging scholar; conference paper or dissertation
- 1-2: Unknown credentials; non-peer-reviewed; blog/opinion

**Accuracy** (Is it well-evidenced?)
- 9-10: Robust methodology; replicable; large sample
- 7-8: Sound methodology; minor limitations acknowledged
- 5-6: Adequate methodology; some gaps
- 3-4: Weak methodology; conclusions overreach evidence
- 1-2: No methodology; anecdotal; unsupported claims

**Purpose** (Why was it written?)
- 9-10: Pure research; no commercial or ideological agenda
- 7-8: Minor institutional bias; disclosed
- 5-6: Moderate bias; useful if noted
- 3-4: Strong bias; use only as counterpoint
- 1-2: Propaganda or marketing; disqualify

**Trust Score** = mean of the five CRAAP dimensions (auto-compute or manual).

**Methodology** = brief label: `RCT`, `quasi-experimental`, `qualitative`, `case study`, `systematic review`, `meta-analysis`, `theoretical`, `mixed-methods`, `N/A`.

## Source Hierarchy Classification

After CRAAP scoring, classify each source into a tier:

| Tier | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **1 — Foundational** | Seminal works that define the field; universally cited | Alexander (1977), Maturana & Varela (1980) |
| **2 — Primary Empirical** | Original research with robust methodology | Peer-reviewed journal articles, large-N studies |
| **3 — Secondary Analytical** | Reviews, meta-analyses, and synthesis papers | Systematic reviews, handbook chapters |
| **4 — Practitioner/Applied** | Industry reports, case studies, dissertations | McKinsey reports, recent EdD/PhD dissertations |
| **5 — Contextual/Gray** | Conference papers, preprints, blog posts, news | arXiv preprints, conference proceedings, trade press |
