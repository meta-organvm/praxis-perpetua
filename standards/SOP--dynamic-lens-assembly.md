# SOP: Dynamic Lens Assembly

**Scope:** system | **Phase:** any | **Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-03-08

## Purpose

Formalize the practice of summoning organizational lenses per-task, interrogating through them, resolving conflicts, and releasing — replacing permanent organizational theory with task-scoped organizational intelligence.

**Source:** [Universal Hierarchy Synthesis](../research/2026-03-08-universal-hierarchy-synthesis.md), §III

## When to Use

- Architectural decisions that affect multiple repos or organs
- Governance questions (promotion, rule creation, enforcement)
- Capacity planning or archival decisions
- Any decision where the "right answer" depends on which framework you use

## When NOT to Use

**The perpetual FRAME anti-pattern.** Summoning lenses is intellectually rewarding — the same cognitive reward that drives construction addiction (E3). The temptation is to keep assembling perspectives instead of acting on them.

The anti-pattern: "Let me also look at this through the lens of..." repeated until the session expires.

**Guard: no more than one assembly per task.** Summon, interrogate, resolve, release, act. The lenses serve the work; the work does not serve the lenses.

## The Assembly Protocol

### Step 1: IDENTIFY the task's stratum (Y6)

| Stratum | Lens Family | Example Lenses |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Implementation detail | Tooling lenses | Chemistry, Infrastructure |
| Architectural decision | Structure lenses | Mathematics, Ecology, Organismal Biology |
| Governance question | Authority lenses | Governance, Academia, Economics |
| Philosophical question | Foundation lenses | Metaphysics, Belief Systems, Noosphere |
| Creative question | Generative lenses | Cultural Expression, Chaos, The Technium |

### Step 2: SUMMON 2-3 lenses appropriate to the stratum

- Never more than 3 simultaneously (cognitive load — Y8)
- Always include one that will CRITIQUE, not just affirm
- Prefer lenses that have high consilience on the question

### Step 3: INTERROGATE through each summoned lens

For each lens, ask:
1. What does this lens **ADD** to understanding?
2. What does this lens **STRIP OUT** as unnecessary?
3. What does this lens **REVEAL** that other lenses miss?
4. What does this lens **DEMAND** as irreducible?

### Step 4: RESOLVE conflicts between summoned lenses

Three resolution patterns (from the synthesis's three conflicts):

| Pattern | When | Resolution |
|---------|------|------------|
| **Dynamic scoping** | Lenses demand opposite actions (e.g., prune vs. preserve) | Both are right at different scopes — apply each where it fits |
| **Phase diagnosis** | Lenses disagree on priority (e.g., governance vs. shipping) | The conflict is temporal, not structural — diagnose the current phase |
| **Stratum separation** | Lenses seem to compete but serve different layers (e.g., internal docs vs. external products) | Apply Y6 — each lens is correct at its stratum |

### Step 5: RELEASE the lenses

- Absorb the insight into the task's output
- Do not carry lens-specific vocabulary into unrelated work
- Do not let a lens persist past its utility
