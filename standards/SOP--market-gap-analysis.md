# SOP: Full-Breath Market-Gap Analysis & Defensive Parrying

## 1. Ontological Purpose
The purpose of this SOP is to provide a standardized, rigorous framework for deconstructing competitors, identifying systemic vulnerabilities, and architecting "parry" strategies that transform competitor failures into our primary market advantages. This is a system-wide document applicable across all ORGANVM projects and is governed by `METADOC--research-standards.md`.

---

## 2. Phase I: Discovery & Deconstruction (Pre-Alpha)
Every target must be analyzed through three layers of abstraction to validate the concept and identify "White Space."

### A. The Skeleton (Technical & Architectural)
*   **Verification Engine:** How does the system confirm truth? (Self-reporting, API, Proof-of-Work, Human Audit).
*   **Technical Deduction Checklist:** For closed-source products, deduce the stack via Job Postings, Integrations, and BuiltWith scans. 
    *   *Theorize when unknown: Explore multiple hypothetical architectural paths to discover superior procession strategies.*
*   **Scalability Bottlenecks:** What breaks when user count increases by 100x?

### B. The Bones (Behavioral & Logical)
*   **Core Driver:** What psychological trigger is being pulled? (Fear of Loss, Status, Altruism).
*   **Friction Analysis:** Where do users drop off? What is too hard to do?

### C. The Meat (Marketing & Execution)
*   **Evidence of Failure:** Hunt for Reddit "rants", Trustpilot 1-star reviews, and "Why I quit" blog posts.
*   **Acquisition Funnel:** How do they find users? (Paid Ads, Viral Loops, B2B Sales).

---

## 3. Phase II: Validation & Gapping (Alpha/Beta)
Transition from discovery to technical and usability validation.

*   **The Shatter Point:** Identify the single failure that makes the product vulnerable.
*   **The Weasel Point:** Identify exactly where the user is most likely to lie because it's easier than complying.
*   **The Trust Gap:** Does the user feel the system is "rigged" or "exploitative"?
*   **Economic Cannibalization:** Does the platform's profit model hurt the user's outcome? (e.g., High commissions).

---

## 4. Phase III: Strategic Parrying (Continuous Growth)
Architect counters that ensure strategic alignment and market post-launch success.

*   **The Technical Parry:** Replace manual sensors with automated "Digital Exhaust."
*   **The Economic Parry:** Replace centralized commissions with decentralized "Bounties."
*   **The Social Parry:** Replace "Soft" social circles with "Hard" adversarial audit networks.

---

## 5. Phase IV: Life Course & Maturity (Omega)
Trace history to inform extension or retirement strategy.

*   **Founders & Funding:** Map the pedigree and capital timeline.
*   **Pivots:** Did they move from B2C to B2B?
*   **Brand Sensitivity:** Audit for naming collisions with high-risk industries (e.g., STYX dark-market collision).
*   **Sanity Checks:** Post-launch research to identify new market segments for expansion or retirement.

---

## 6. Phase V: Synthesis & Iteration
Aggregate findings into a **Market Matrix** and extend the product's lifespan through an "Iteration" stage even after the decline phase begins.
