# METADOC: Architectural Typology & Research Standards

## 1. Ontological Foundation
All research within the ORGANVM ecosystem is governed by the principles of **Metatheoretical Synthesis** and **First Principles Reconstruction**. We do not summarize; we engage in **Archetypal Systems Analysis** to extract the "Deep Structure" (the eternal essence) beneath the "Surface Structure" (temporal style), establishing invariant "Covenants" for creation.

Our framework traverses three temporal pillars:
1. **Historiography (The Past):** What was.
2. **Ontology (The Present):** What is.
3. **Teleology (The Future):** What must be.

We operate as authors of a **Paradigm**, creating Transformative Systems designed to actively force the future into a specific, resilient shape.

## 5. Source Standards

### The 5 Cs Framework (Mandatory)
Every analysis must apply the five actions. This is not optional guidance — it is a minimum standard:
1. **Cite:** Credit authors correctly and establish the historical timeframe.
2. **Compare:** Highlight similarities in findings, methodologies, or research questions across studies.
3. **Contrast:** Identify where findings or theories disagree and explore why.
4. **Critique:** Evaluate research design soundness and whether conclusions are supported.
5. **Connect:** Relate existing literature directly to the research problem and methodology.

### CRAAP Test (Minimum Bar)
Every source must be evaluated on Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, and Purpose. Trust Score >= 6 for inclusion. Procedure defined in `SOP--source-evaluation-and-bibliography.md` (Phase II).

### Reference Volume Target
A doctoral-standard bibliography typically cites 150-200 references. ORGANVM projects should target this range for major research efforts. Smaller-scope research may adjust proportionally but must document the rationale.

### Iterative Mandate
A bibliography is "never finished" until the research is submitted. Continuously update as new research publishes. Saturation is checked, not assumed.

## 6. Pillar II: Ontology (The Present — Lifecycle-Phase Research Triggers)

Research is a continuous loop synchronized with the product lifecycle. Each phase triggers specific SOP invocations:

| Lifecycle Phase | Research Focus | SOP Invocations |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Pre-Alpha (Discovery)** | Market validation, concept testing, white-space identification | Source Evaluation (full), Market-Gap (Phase I-II), Typological (Phase I-III) |
| **Alpha (Internal Validation)** | Technical stability, feature completeness, architectural integrity | Structural Integrity Audit (Phase I-III), Autopoietic Diagnostics (Phase I) |
| **Beta (External Validation)** | User feedback, usability, preliminary pricing, performance | Market-Gap (Phase II-III), Source Evaluation (iteration) |
| **Live/Growth (Post-Launch)** | Market penetration, CX measurement, new segment identification | Strategic Foresight (Phase I-II), Market-Gap (Phase III) |
| **Maturity** | Extension strategy, efficiency optimization, competitive moat | Autopoietic Diagnostics (full), Strategic Foresight (Phase IV-V) |
| **Decline/Omega** | Retirement vs. extension decision, spare parts strategy, knowledge preservation | Autopoietic Diagnostics (full), Backcasting (revised Omega) |

**Key performance drivers:**
- **Strategic alignment:** Research ensures product strategy remains evidence-based from kickoff through Omega.
- **Risk reduction:** 70-80% of products fail before launch due to poor market research or lack of demand.
- **Continuous iteration:** The "Iteration" stage extends the product's lifespan even after decline begins.

## 7. Pillar III: Teleology (The Future — Strategic Foresight)
To move from historical synthesis to concrete future execution without drifting into speculative fiction, we employ **Prospective Theory** and **Strategic Foresight**.

### A. Environmental Scanning (STEEP)
Map the Social, Technological, Economic, Environmental, and Political drivers. Track "Weak Signals" and "Megatrends" rather than guessing.

**Operationalized in:** `SOP--strategic-foresight-and-futures.md` (Phase I).

### B. Scenario Planning & Transition Design
Build 3-4 plausible futures based on critical uncertainties. Stress-test our Covenants and Products against these scenarios to identify necessary "Pivots and Dodges." Treat the shift from the current Regime to the preferred future Landscape as a design problem.

**Operationalized in:** `SOP--strategic-foresight-and-futures.md` (Phase II, Phase V).

### C. Backcasting (The Concrete Plan)
Start with the "Omega" (desired future state) and work backward to the present. Generate a high-fidelity Roadmap detailing the infrastructure and covenants required at specific temporal milestones to reach the Omega.

**Operationalized in:** `SOP--strategic-foresight-and-futures.md` (Phase IV).

### D. Causal Layered Analysis (CLA)
Analyze the future across four depths:
1.  **Litany:** Surface-level trends (the news).
2.  **Social Causes:** Systemic drivers (economics/policy).
3.  **Worldview:** Deep-seated beliefs supporting the system.
4.  **Myth/Metaphor:** The unconscious stories (the "Alchemy") that, when changed, alter the future form.

**Operationalized in:** `SOP--strategic-foresight-and-futures.md` (Phase III).
