# The AI Conductor's Guide to Shipping Real Software

> Ingested from ChatGPT Compass artifact. Practical guide for non-traditional developers (artist-academic-rhetorician) orchestrating AI systems. Covers process frameworks (Personal Kanban, Shape Up, PARA, indie hacker philosophy), the vibe coding revolution and its limits (Karpathy→agentic engineering, spec-first workflow, multi-model ensembles, documented failure modes), monetization paths (productized consulting, cohort courses, YouTube funnels, communities), the "triple-serving" strategy (product + portfolio + publication via research-creation), and a 50-70 hour learning roadmap (Git, debugging, project structure, CI/CD). Key insight: the non-traditional background is the asset, not the liability.

## Relationship to ORGANVM North Star

This is the **practical playbook** companion to the academic framework. Where the SDLC Framework provides theoretical grounding, this guide provides actionable methods for shipping. The "triple-serving" architecture (build one thing that simultaneously serves as product, portfolio, and academic output) directly maps to ORGANVM's multi-organ model.

| North Star Pillar | Guide Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Anti-Stagnation Governance | Fixed time, variable scope (Shape Up's core mantra): constraints enable creativity. WIP limits force finishing before starting. MoSCoW scoping with explicit "Won't-have" lists. |
| Ethical Human-in-Loop | Spec-first workflow: human writes specification, AI generates code, human reviews. "Let go emotionally of the idea that you are there to write code. Your role is engineering and product manager of an AI team." |
| Individual→Enterprise Amplification | Research-creation framework: one body of work serving three masters (revenue, portfolio, scholarship). Casey Reas model where open-source software IS art practice IS teaching IS research IS commercial product. |
