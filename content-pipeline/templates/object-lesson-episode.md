## 2. Production Structure

The Object Lessons hybrid format alternates between narrated analysis and non-narrated supercut sequences.

### COLD OPEN (0:00 - 1:00)

**Type:** Non-narrated supercut montage
**Purpose:** Immediate visual impact -- the most striking, surprising, or beautiful appearances of the object across cinema history

- [ ] 8-15 clips selected for maximum visual diversity
- [ ] Pacing builds energy (slow cuts to rapid)
- [ ] No text overlays, no narration -- pure cinema
- [ ] Music bed established
- [ ] Final clip creates natural transition into thesis

**Clip count:** ___
**Music cue:** ___

### THESIS (1:00 - 2:00)

**Type:** Narrated introduction
**Purpose:** Frame the question -- what does this object mean in cinema? Why does it keep appearing?

- [ ] Opens with a provocative claim or question
- [ ] Establishes the object's ubiquity across film history
- [ ] Previews the journey without spoiling the analysis
- [ ] Hooks the viewer into staying for the full essay
- [ ] Transition into historical survey feels natural

**Key line:** ___

### HISTORICAL SURVEY (2:00 - 8:00)

**Type:** Narrated analysis with supporting clips
**Purpose:** The core essay -- tracing the object's meaning across directors, eras, and genres

Structure options (choose one or blend):
- **Chronological:** Silent era to present, showing how meaning evolves
- **Thematic:** Grouped by what the object represents (innocence, power, death, etc.)
- **Dialectical:** Contrasting pairs (sacred vs. profane, luxury vs. poverty)

- [ ] 3-5 major sections/themes identified
- [ ] Each section has 8-15 supporting clips
- [ ] Narration leads, clips illustrate (not the reverse)
- [ ] Cross-director connections highlighted
- [ ] At least one unexpected or obscure example per section
- [ ] Pacing varies (dense analysis alternates with breathing room)

### ANALYSIS (8:00 - 10:00)

**Type:** Narrated synthesis
**Purpose:** Emergent patterns -- what does the full survey reveal that individual examples don't?

- [ ] Patterns identified across the survey (recurring contexts, director obsessions, cultural shifts)
- [ ] At least one insight that could only emerge from seeing all appearances together
- [ ] Connection to broader filmmaking craft (cinematography, production design, mise-en-scene)
- [ ] Avoids being reductive -- acknowledges complexity and contradiction
- [ ] Sets up the emotional resonance of the closing montage

### CLOSING MONTAGE (10:00 - 11:00)

**Type:** Non-narrated supercut
**Purpose:** Recontextualize -- after the analysis, the viewer sees these images differently

- [ ] 10-20 clips, mix of previously shown and new
- [ ] Pacing and music build to an emotional crescendo
- [ ] Final image is carefully chosen (lingers, resonates)
- [ ] No narration -- let the accumulated meaning do the work
- [ ] Distinct feel from cold open (cold open = curiosity, closing = understanding)

**Final image:** ___

### CTA + END SCREEN (11:00+)

**Type:** Narrated call to action + YouTube end screen elements
**Purpose:** Convert viewers into subscribers and drive to next video

- [ ] "Which object should we explore next?" (engagement prompt)
- [ ] Subscribe reminder (brief, not desperate)
- [ ] End screen: next video suggestion, subscribe button, playlist link
- [ ] Pinned comment prepared (question + link to related episode)
