The door IS the cut. Every film edit is a threshold -- we leave one space and enter another. The dissolve is a door slowly opening. The smash cut is a door kicked open. The fade to black is a door closing. Cinema's most fundamental operation is architecturally a door.

From Dorothy opening her farmhouse door onto the Technicolor wonder of Oz to Michael Corleone's office door closing on Kay -- from HAL refusing to open the pod bay doors to the raptor that learned to turn a handle -- the door is cinema's most common prop, most invisible architecture, and most honest metaphor. It frames the shot, divides the world into here and there, and forces characters to choose which side they're on.
