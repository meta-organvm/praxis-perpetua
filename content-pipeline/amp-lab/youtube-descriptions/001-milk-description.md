Milk is the most dangerous drink in cinema. From Hitchcock's glowing poisoned glass to Kubrick's Korova Milk Bar to Jordan Peele's Sunken Place -- every glass of milk on screen is doing something.

In a hundred years of filmmaking, almost no character drinks milk casually. When a villain orders milk, they're performing innocence as dominance. When a killer drinks milk alone, it's their last tether to humanity. When milk is extracted from bodies, the domestic becomes industrial. This is the story of cinema's most loaded beverage -- and once you see the pattern, you can't unsee it.
