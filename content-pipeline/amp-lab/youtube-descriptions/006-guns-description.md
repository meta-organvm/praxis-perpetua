The movie camera was born as a weapon. In 1882, Etienne-Jules Marey built a rifle-shaped device that "shot" twelve frames per second. We still "shoot" films. We compose "shots." We "aim" cameras. The gun and the camera share DNA -- and cinema has spent 120 years pointing both at audiences.

No other object in cinema is simultaneously this essential and this contested. The gun built the Western, the gangster picture, the action franchise. It is the engine of half the stories Hollywood has ever told. It is also the object whose meaning has split irreparably -- the instrument of justice AND the instrument of massacre, carried in the same hand, in the same frame. From the outlaw who fired at the audience in 1903 to John Wick's gun-fu ballets, this is the story of cinema's most loaded prop.
